Outsource Design at Your Own Risk

Oct 29th, 2012

It was only whilst in the middle of warfare that I could really comprehend the perils of outsourcing design.

There is marginally acceptable design and then there is design for optimization.

Don’t confuse the two.

No startup needs the latter, but to disregard the former would be to walk into the jaws of peril as any pixels out of place make you look shady and leaves your potential customers suspicious about the quality of your offerings. Build quality, build fast. Use design sparingly and only when it matters. And no matter what, do not get carried away.

Make sure you build from the inside out. What this means is figure out your model and achieve product market fit before adding the icing on the cake. Make sure you’ve got a foundation before buying the furniture and going gangbusters decorating.

Why investors don’t look at the product, they only care about numbers. Get the numbers.

The Market of Bored People

Oct 29th, 2012

7:35pm. Everyone has gone home. I’m still here. I didn’t finish my work until 7pm today and couldn’t wait to crack open TB to keep the momentum going.

I thought it odd for a startup to be empty after 6p. Surely this couldn’t be normal. I’d always envisioned startups to be where people hung out until late at night but apparently I was wrong.

Changed the reply from a button to a tear-off. Like the sort you would see on the Stanford community wall where folks posted if they had something to sell, or was looking for, etc. The kind you’d browse if you weren’t terribly busy but perhaps waiting for someone in a lobby.

It was gnawing on me. If I built it, would they come? And how much of it depended on the experience since the actual necessity of this whole thing was tenuous to begin with? I’d use it, but as I’d deemed earlier I’m not really in the mainstream. Question was how many others are there like me out there?

Just a Few Ideas

Oct 26th, 2012

Producer. Instigator. Creator. Artist. God.

  2. You are on a mission. It is like being in space. Only you’re on the ground. Sort of like an invisible spaceship, that no one can see you building except for yourself.
  3. You like ideas which are easily testable and which you deem would be “cool if it existed”
  4. You like ideas which disrupt traditional structures.
  5. You like participatory culture.
  6. You like to make things happen.

Why the Experience Can Make or Break Your Product

Oct 21st, 2012

Emphasis.

I was staring at my application after my 3rd glass of red wine, hoping for one of those aha! moments to hit.

Sure, enough, one did. It suddenly occurred to me that the feed should be a true feed from my community irrespective of who was contributing at the moment. So the fact that the name was the first thing I saw actually distracted me from the thing which I wanted the user to focus most on, which was what was being asked rather than who was doing the asking. I wanted to calibrate everyone so that I treated each person the same, all people with whom I’d actually met before, and somewhat vetted as real live people.

I removed the name of the person from the first line and check it again. Well, there had to be some way of responding. So I added the person to the last 3rd piece of information behind the actual thing which was being discussed and the time at which it was posted and de-emphasized the size. So you could see who posted it, but it wasn’t distracting in any way. The end result? You treated everyone the same.

Boom.
