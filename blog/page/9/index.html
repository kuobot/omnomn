Ode to Chocolate Covered Gummy Bears

Jul 31st, 2012

I can’t remember when I was first introduced to these delectable little guys. It was love at first bite. Seriously. I could eat a whole bagful whilst programming furiously away. Tonight I decided to scour the internet to find where I could procure chocolate covered gummy bears… in bulk.

After searching high and low for about 30 minutes or so, I finally found what I decided was the best price to least sketchiest vendor ratio. I had to refrain from selecting the option for 25 pounds of chocolate covered gummy bears for $148.75. As I began checking out, I noticed a little countdown timer pop up in the lower right corner of the screen letting me know that if I checked out within the next five minutes, I would save an extra 5%. What a good idea for countering shopping cart abandonment, I thought. Not that I would abandon my chocolate covered gummy bears.

One reviewer warned of high shipping charges. Hmm… perhaps I ought to buy more in that case, I thought. So you know those times you get stuck on the internet? Well, I’m having one of those moments right now. It became painfully obvious to me I wasn’t going to be making it out of here within the next five minutes. I mean, here I am just innocently trying to buy a shitload ton of chocolate covered gummy bears, and I am waiting patiently for the shipping rates to appear so I can make an informed decision as to whether I ought to get the five pound package or the ten. I almost gave up thinking the site was stuck. Finally, after 5 minutes of watching the brown spinner go round and round the charges showed up. FedEx was going to be $13 for 10 pounds and … (hrm, another 5 minutes, perhaps I ought to go take a shower while I wait). Ok, out of the shower and still waiting. I gave up and called it a night, and decided I would call them in the morning.

Three thoughts were running though my mind at this point: 1) seriously, how hard can it be to buy chocolate covered gummy bears? 2) perhaps I ought to wait until closer to Christmas to justify a larger purchase, and 3) if anyone within driving distance from the middle of SF wants in, let me know.
