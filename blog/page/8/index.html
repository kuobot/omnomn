On Building a Team

Aug 1st, 2012

I learned so much about how to build a team from my startup. Or rather, how not to build a team.

Despite what was said in public town halls, there was an underlying current of distrust of management. The root cause of this was that the founder kept promising things he failed to deliver upon. It was ironic for the team to speak of brand management to our customers while our leader was slowly eroding his brand amongst his employees. While he may not be doing it intentionally, I told our head of sales, he needs to stop. Otherwise it just looks like a bunch of lip service. That coupled with his innate sarcastic tone and condensation it was a formula headed straight for disaster.

There are many ways to build a company. But there is only one way you ought to treat people. Aspire to lead, not manage. (management philosophies)

Discover Your Voice

Aug 1st, 2012

To get good at something, you just have to do it. Over and over again. One of the sales reps at my startup company taught me this. It’s ok for your pitch not to be so good at first. The most important thing is that you do it over and over and over again to find your voice. Practice makes perfect, and sometimes you’ve just got to dive in and put your ass in the saddle. And go for it.

Do this diligently, and soon you’ll find that the words will flow automatically. Pitch to your dog. Pitch to your cat. Pitch to your 90-year old grandma. Pitch to your 12 year old neighbor. Pitch to a plant. Pitch to yourself in the mirror.

The same thing goes with design. Every artist has their own voice, characteristics that make something uniquely their own. If you’re just starting out, simply make designs. Lots and lots of them. Create, critique, and create again. Learn how to do it just a little bit better the next time.
