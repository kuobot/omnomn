Ask for Help

Oct 21st, 2012

I like to play what if.

What if you were unafraid to ask for help? Our culture of independence has stifled the very thought of asking someone for help. Figure it out yourself is the going norm. What if we asking for help became the norm, and we lived in a world where people helped one another? As one gets older, one has less and less time to simply “hang out” but finds oneself to be more focused in his or her efforts and quite a bit more selective in who we spend our time with. Time becomes more scarce, but should not preclude one from helping another should one be in a position to do so.

Question is, how does one know their friend needs help? And would the friend ask for help, and for which situations, and somewhat publicly as they would not know who might have the answer to their question. In networking, when one meets another, I am frequently surprised at how few people utilize the opportunity to ask for help, but rather squander it in idle talk and meaningless banter. And then when it’s time to say goodbye, the brief interaction fades away quite rapidly, time spent and lost forever should there be no record of the transaction even happening in the first place.

Imagine a helping application. This application would be used to capture these spontaneous collisions betweeen 2 people, as a way of eliciting at least some potential future value from the meeting in exchange for the time spent. The hypothesis here is that there is some value in physical, face-to-face meetings. There is zero awkwardness in the transaction, as there’s no promise or expectation or judgement as to how each defines the term “friend” or “colleague”. The interaction is simply captured, and each goes upon his or her merry way. But there is validation that this is an actual person whom you met and should they be brave enough to ask their community of loose acquaintances for help, they might just as well get what they are looking for.

But if you think of the entire world as operating on that local maximum graph, you’re on that same little hump. Starting a brand new one is far riskier and you’d be venturing into unknown territory. This is where blue oceans are created.
