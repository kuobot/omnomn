Doing the Startup Thing

Nov 24th, 2012

San Francisco is a sort of a microcosm in that people from all over the world flock here to “do the startup thing”. Since moving here, I’ve met so many people who’ve traveled far and wide from Europe, Australia, the Midwest, China and more. Why? This is the place where magic happens. Perhaps if you come here, some of the magic will rub off on you too. It sort of reminds me of the time I was in Dubai, where there was no such thing as a shortage of land. They simply created it. Here in the Valley, there’s no shortage of ideas. You simply come up with more. There’s no shame in trying and failing.

The real shame is in not trying at all.

This is what the New Web is all about. Less about what technology can do than what we can do using technology. When you take a careful look, you realize that it’s not the idea that’s the important thing. The idea is secondary. The primary goal here is to simply making something happen. Anything.

As long as we prove something of ourselves.

My college roommate gave me a framed quote from Johann Wolfgang von Goethe which stated “Whatever you can do, or dream you can do, begin it. Boldness has genius, power, and magic in it. Begin it now.”

What the German novelist, dramatist, poet, humanist, scientist, and philosopher was the fundamental fuel of startups. The spirit of the times ignites within each and every one of us the urge to make some sort of impact upon the world. We live in a modern-day renaissance inciting passion and fervor and sense of urgency which is the most exciting thing about being alive.

Here and now.
