Doing the Startup Thing

Nov 24th, 2012

San Francico is a sort of a microcosm in that people from all over the world flock here to “do the startup thing”. I’ve met people from Europe, Australia, the Midwest, China and more. This is the place where magic happens. Perhaps if you come here, some of the magic will rub off on you too. It sort of reminds me of Dubai, where there was no such thing as a shortage of land. They simply created it. Here in the Valley, there’s no shortage of ideas. You simply come up with more. There’s no shame in trying and failing. The real shame is in not trying at all.

This is largely what the New Web is all about. Less about what technology can do, than what we can do with technology, which is to prove something of ourselves.

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (28 August 1749 – 22 March 1832) was a German novelist, dramatist, poet, humanist, scientist, philosopher, and for ten years chief minister of state at Weimar. He stated most eloquently about the human spirit we witness today:

“Until one is committed, there is hesitancy, the chance to draw back. Concerning all acts of initiative (and creation), there is one elementary truth, the ignorance of which kills countless ideas and splendid plans: that the moment one definitely commits oneself, then Providence moves too. All sorts of things occur to help one that would never otherwise have occurred. A whole stream of events issues from the decision, raising in one’s favor all manner of unforeseen incidents and meetings and material assistance, which no man could have dreamed would have come his way. Whatever you can do, or dream you can do, begin it. Boldness has genius, power, and magic in it. Begin it now.”

There is no doubt that startups are fashionable these days, and for good reason. It is more of the spirit of the times than anything else, a renaissance of sorts inciting passion and fervor and sense of urgency which is the most exciting thing about living here, now.
