Startup Is Fashion

Nov 24th, 2012

Dear Mom,

You know how in LA you would hear people talking about blue jeans and sporting the latest fashionable large handbags and sunglasses? Well, instead here you hear about the latest idea, what the experience will be like, and plans to gain traction. There is definitely a fashion undercurrent in the air here as far as being “startuppy” goes.I was thinking about this the other day and I quickly identified a dozen in order to give you an idea of what it looks like, as it may be difficult for you to comprehend without actually being here.

Here they are in no particular order:

  1. Gone: Heavy spec writing.
    Instead: Lightweight, standups instead.

  2. Gone: The unknown corporation.
    Instead: The personable touch.

  3. Gone: Ignoring the customer
    Instead: The customer is king.

  4. Gone: Poor user experiences
    Instead: Spotlight on the user experience. Whimsy, personality, fun. Character. This is why copyrighting is so important these days.

  5. Gone: Just build it
    Instead: Figure out what you will build and market and sell it first. Then build it

  6. Gone: Silos of demarcatation
    Instead: Integrated, small design-build teams who can turn on a dime.

  7. Gone: Boring jobs
    Instead: You can have your cake and eat it too

  8. Gone: The corporate zombie
    Instead: The enlightened, self-motivated employee

  9. Gone: Playing by the rules
    Intead: Making the rules. Or in some cases, breaking them first

  10. Gone: Believing you can’t.
    Instead: Believing you can.

  11. Gone: Traditional models and institutions
    Instead: Forging your own way

  12. Gone: Traditional glass ceilings
    Instead: Fuck you money

It seems these characteristics can be found in organizations large or small, and there’s no reason why one cannot implement one or a few today regardless of what they do for work or what position they are in.
