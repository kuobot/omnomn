Month 14

Nov 14th, 2012

Over drinks one night, I asked my friend what he’d learned from his startup.

I think back and I have gained so much experience from the past 14 months I wouldn’t trade it for the world. Don’t get me wrong, it was pretty rough. I’m not a natural hustler, and I was definitely no coder.

The top 10 things I learned were:

  1. Know why you’re doing what you’re doing. I sort of fell into local online advertising. It wasn’t a natural passion of mine admittedly, but I was there to learn come hell or high water. All I knew was that I wanted the startup experience, and that was exactly what I’d got.

  2. When you love what you do you don’t do it for the money. This is the #1 reason companies today are having a tough time finding development talent. Why the hell would you help raise someone else’s baby when you could have your own?

  3. Always be learning. The day you stop learning is probably the day you should leave.

  4. Ruthlessly prioritize.

  5. Most of all, you will change yourself.

  6. Forget having hobbies.

  7. Your role shifts from being consumer to creator.

  8. Always be shipping.

  9. Question everything.

  10. Have no fear.
