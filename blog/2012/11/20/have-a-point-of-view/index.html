Have a Point of View

Nov 20th, 2012

The best products have a point of view. The strongest brands do.

I was frustrated at our lack of willingness to be decisive about big decisions. Nothing is irreversible. And why we were treating it as such was beyond me.

Every product, every startup, ought to have a strong POV. My POV for omnomn was that people should help other people more, and that people ought to ask for help more. The hypothesis was that there simply was no mechanism by which people could easily see what others needed. And if you don’t know what the person sitting across from you needed, how could you help them?

The shape of behavior is changing, and the internet was largely fueling this phenomenon. Not that this happens overnight. You can think of it like erosion. If you look at the rocks on the coast of XXX what you see is marvelous formations with sensuous and curvaceous shapes.
