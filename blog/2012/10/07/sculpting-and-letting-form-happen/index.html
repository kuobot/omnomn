Sculpting and Letting Form Happen

Oct 7th, 2012

Removed photos b/c they were ruining my experience as the designer. Gravatars way too inconsistent. Screw this, I said, lets just remove them. I knew it was en vogue to be as visual as possible; however with the sample data I’d pre-loaded with actual users whom I knew, I wasn’t having a very good experience.

Unless I could control the quality of the photos taken (ie. they were all mug shots taken with precise constraints) the better option for right now was simply to remove the photos altogether.

I contemplated my belly button for a second. What would happen if I were to standardize all the photos? Say, everyone looked super professional, or everyone looked fun and interesting, etc. You remove that competitive advantage, and there’s one less variable one is likely to be distracted by.

Another service that would be really interesting I just thought of (which I’d picked up and turned over before) would be a resume/career counseling service for job-seekers. Amy is going to one right now. How large is that market? And can a platform be created to help many vs the few it’s currently limited to? What if everyone had really super awesome-looking resumes that made it easy on the recruiters by using best practices for everyone? No more bad resumes, there is no such thing. Only poorly presented ones.

What is the price of a career counselor?

Tangential mind-wandering. Jaunts along the way that you can spin off. I’m an opportunist. It’s why whenever I see a sale I double-down. Ben makes fun of me for that. Hey man, I said, you’re lucky you’re dating an opportunist. Would you rather I go out and buy things full-price? He quickly backpedaled. See? You just have to make them see the light.
