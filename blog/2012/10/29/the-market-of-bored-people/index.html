The Market of Bored People

Oct 29th, 2012

7:35pm. Everyone has gone home. I’m still here. I didn’t finish my work until 7pm today and couldn’t wait to crack open TB to keep the momentum going.

I thought it odd for a startup to be empty after 6p. Surely this couldn’t be normal. I’d always envisioned startups to be where people hung out until late at night but apparently I was wrong.

Changed the reply from a button to a tear-off. Like the sort you would see on the Stanford community wall where folks posted if they had something to sell, or was looking for, etc. The kind you’d browse if you weren’t terribly busy but perhaps waiting for someone in a lobby.

It was gnawing on me. If I built it, would they come? And how much of it depended on the experience since the actual necessity of this whole thing was tenuous to begin with? I’d use it, but as I’d deemed earlier I’m not really in the mainstream. Question was how many others are there like me out there?
