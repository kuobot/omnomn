How to Get Dimensions From a Flat Photoshop File

Oct 3rd, 2012

Ok, so I must have spent at least a good half hour this morning trying to figure out how to find out the dimensions of a goddamn rectangle in Photoshop. That’s 30 minutes of my life which I will never get back, thanks to our designers starting out in Photoshop.

All I have to say to anyone who is building web products is this. If you’re at all concerned with the costs incurred with designing and building an app, you should not start out in Photoshop. Especially when your operating in the lean agile modality.

So why do people still design web products using Photoshop? I have a number of theories. First, they are familiar with the tool. But these guys will eventually all die off, if they refuse to reinvent themselves.

Second, the over-emphasis placed upon optimization and creating super fancy images out the gate. Sure, I readily admit these are nice and necessary at some point. I would argue that these don’t move the needle. Especially at the beginning. Indeed, your precious resources are better spent buying plane tickets or starbucks lattes for potential users willing to give you feedback.

But enough of my rant. So you don’t have to suffer as I did, here’s who I found the dimensions.

  1. Find the object you want by toggling the little eyeball in the layers panel.
  2. Right click -> Convert to Smart Object
  3. Cmd+C then Cmd+N to see the dimensions
  4. Cmd+V if you want to paste to make sure you did it right.

Enjoy!
