Build Technology for Humans

Sep 12th, 2012

Don’t get me wrong. Knowledge is nice. It is useful. But never, ever mistake it for wisdom.

What’s the difference, you ask? Knowledge changes. Wisdom never does. Back at Stanford, I vividly recall standing in the ME101 room in the Terman Engineering Room looking at a plaque on the wall engraved with a single quote by former professor of design Terry Winblad. It read:

“I chose to study people because human nature changes much more slowly than technology.”

The pace at which technology is changing today is fairly rapid. But if you take a look at humanity, all you see are constants. Sure, the way in which we communicate is fundamentally altered with social media and technology enabling interactions with others in different ways online. I would argue that technology is great for discovery purposes in that sense, but still there is no substitute for face to face interactions. So much is lost which is not captured, the facial expressions, the fact that the personality can be easily altered, just to name a few.

Technology ought to be used as an enhancement to our normal life, rather than a substitute for. The danger lies in using technology as a form of escapism, entering into a virtual reality sort of world.

So seek to learn and aquire knowledge, but always remember why you’re doing so – and don’t forsake wisdom in the process.

Lets focus on what we’re doing now and make that bombproof. And for gods sake, come up with a design that is flexible enough to scale as we grow.
