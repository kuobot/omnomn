On Rickety Roller Coasters

Sep 16th, 2012

Phil Knight coined the phrase “Just do it”.

When I first started out, I was a total newbie. I had no idea what it took to build a startup. Sure, it’s easy. Everyone else is doing it. Boy, was I wrong. Nobody tells you to expect to fail 999 times before you hit it big. Why should they? No one wants to hear that plain ol’ hard work wins out at the end. They all hear what they want to believe, which is that you work for a couple weeks and accidentally build a company that is worth a billion dollars. Erm, not exactly. The story is repeated often enough that eventually becomes truncated in order to boost the shock and awe factor. Don’t let this fool you.

According to XX startups are like the ricketiest roller coasters you’ll ever ride. That is 100% true. If the ride you’re on feels like Dumbo, you’re probably not doing the right things. Which means unless you’ve got deep pockets, chances are you’ll run out of money before you hit the gas pedal.

The weird part is, if you haven’t gone though it once before and “been there”, chances are you cannot possibly fathom what it takes to make it. In fact, the closest analogy I can think of is one where you’re running a marathon. Full of sprints.

Ben and I went to Six Flags Discovery Kingdom last weekend. We rode Superman.
